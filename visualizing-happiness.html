Visualizing World Happiness

Role: Data Scientist, Designer
Collaborators: Lila Finch, Daniel Frost, Matilda Whitemore, Adam Siefkes
Type: Information Visualization

Overview

Every year, the United Nations releases a ranking of the world's happiest countries using data from the Gallup World Poll. The ranking is calculated using factors such as GDP, life expectancy, generosity, social support, freedom, and corruption. For an undergraduate course in information visualization, my team hoped to share this data with the public by creating an physical interactive information system. We wanted users to be able to gain insight into interesting relationships, trends, and outliers through discovery and exploration. Our hopes were that through this process, we could make data more accessible to the public, as well as inspire empathy and understanding. Our project was put on display in the front lobby of the ATLAS Institute at the University of Colorado, Boulder.

Designing the Visualization

We turned to the literature to understand previous successful data exploration systems. We brainstormed and sketched design concepts, and created a blueprint.

Construction & Assembly

We cleaned and imported the data using Python, and created a program to regulate LED lights. We used a laser woodcutter to create a map of the world, a table saw to cut bars, and a lot of hot glue to bring everything together. We stayed up all night and assembled the visualization in the ATLAS Institute lobby.

Python, electronics, woodworking

Results & Lessons Learned

We observed individuals engaging with the visualization in a naturalistic setting, and noted the unique discoveries they were making. We learned how to make data accessible to the general public and wrote a final report.
